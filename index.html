Research

With the existence of over 100 types of cancer, and the similarity of its symptoms with a myriad of other diseases, the misdiagnosis of cancer has become a prevalent phenomenon. According to a report by All Can,... 4 in 10 patients receive an incorrect diagnosis before a correct one. In India especially, the lack of registered pathologists has put patients in danger through malpractice and misdiagnosis. Although various non-invasive procedures have been used, histopathology studies are still considered to be the gold standard when it comes to cancer detection and analysis. In the case of ovarian cancer, which originates in the ovaries and spreads to the abdomen and the pelvis, early detection is difficult but crucial. Ovarian cancer causes the fifth highest cancer-related deaths among women. 1 in 75 women will be diagnosed for it, and 1 in 100 people with ovarian cancer will die. However, if diagnosed early, the treatment tends to be successful 85% of the time. We have been working on deep learning techniques to ensure early detection and treatment, even in the case of cancers that are silent in the early stages, like ovarian cancer. Cancers manifest in different people in different ways, and our approach to treatment procedures reflects that, with an emphasis on personalised medicine

Solution

Convolutional neural networks (CNN) is a type of deep learning network that is primarily used to classify images, and applications of CNNs range from self-driving cars to discovery of drugs. Using this technology,... we have developed a diagnostic tool that is capable of diagnosing ovarian cancer, identifying what stage the cancer is in, and the mutations associated with it, all within a time period of just five minutes! There is no gatekeeping of medical information, as all the data would be readily available to the patient, and not at the discretion of an oncologist. This would be a valuable tool for pathologists attempting to make accurate decisions. Currently, we are working on developing this tool to include various other types of cancer as well. We are also researching the potential personalised therapy recommendations for ovarian cancer. Personalised medicine has just made a foray into treatment options, and our focus is on automating the recommendations process. .
